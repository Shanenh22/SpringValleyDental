Complete Dentures for Total Tooth Replacement

Complete dentures are removable appliances that replace all teeth in the upper or lower jaw. They are typically made from acrylic or sometimes metal‑based materials and are designed to closely mimic the appearance of natural teeth and gums. Full dentures restore your ability to eat comfortably and speak clearly while supporting your facial structure to prevent the sunken appearance that can occur with tooth loss.

Partial Dentures for Several Missing Teeth

Partial dentures are an excellent solution when you have some healthy natural teeth remaining. These removable appliances fill the gaps left by missing teeth and are held in place by clasps that attach to your existing teeth or by precision attachments for a more aesthetic result. Partials prevent your remaining teeth from shifting and help maintain proper bite alignment.

Types of Dentures We Offer

We provide several denture options to meet your specific needs and budget. Conventional dentures are made after all teeth are removed and tissues have healed, typically 4–6 weeks after extractions. Immediate dentures are placed right after tooth extraction so you aren’t without teeth, but because gums shrink as they heal they often require adjustments and may eventually need to be remade for a better fit. Implant‑supported dentures offer superior stability and retention by anchoring to dental implants.

The Denture Process

Creating your custom dentures involves several appointments to ensure proper fit and function. We begin with detailed impressions and measurements of your mouth. Multiple fittings allow us to perfect the fit, bite, and appearance before finalizing your dentures. Our goal is to create dentures that fit comfortably, function well, and look natural. The entire process typically takes 4-6 weeks for conventional dentures.

Implant-Supported Dentures

For enhanced stability and function, consider implant-supported dentures. Dental implants are placed in your jawbone to serve as anchors for your dentures. This option provides significantly better retention, allows you to eat a wider variety of foods, and helps preserve your jawbone. Implant-supported dentures can be removable or fixed permanently in place, depending on your preferences and needs.

Adjusting to Your New Dentures

There's an adjustment period when you first receive dentures. Initially, they may feel bulky or loose, and eating and speaking may take practice. Start with soft foods cut into small pieces and gradually introduce more challenging textures. Reading aloud can help you adapt to speaking with dentures. Most people adjust within a few weeks, and we'll make any necessary adjustments to improve comfort and fit.

Caring for Your Dentures

Proper denture care ensures they last longer and function better. Remove and rinse dentures after eating to remove food particles. Clean them daily with a soft-bristled brush and denture cleaner—never use regular toothpaste, which can be abrasive. Soak dentures overnight in water or denture solution to keep them moist. Continue caring for your gums and any remaining teeth with regular brushing and dental visits.

When to Consider Denture Replacement

Dentures typically last 5-7 years before needing replacement, though regular adjustments and relines can extend their life. Over time, your mouth changes shape, and dentures may become loose or uncomfortable. Signs you need new dentures include difficulty eating, slipping during speech, mouth sores, or visible wear. We'll monitor your dentures during regular checkups and recommend replacement when necessary.
