What is Gum Disease?

Gum disease begins with gingivitis—red, swollen gums that bleed easily. If left untreated, it can progress to periodontitis, a serious infection that destroys the soft tissue and bone supporting your teeth. Early intervention is key to stopping gum disease and preventing tooth loss.

Symptoms of Gum Disease

  • Gums that bleed when brushing or flossing
  • Persistent bad breath
  • Receding gumline or longer‑looking teeth
  • Swollen, tender or dark red gums
  • Loose or shifting teeth

Our Periodontal Therapy

We begin with a comprehensive gum evaluation to determine the severity of your condition. Treatments often start with deep cleanings (scaling and root planing) to remove plaque and tartar below the gumline. Scaling removes deposits above and below the gumline, while root planing smooths the tooth roots to discourage bacteria from reattaching. The procedure is performed under local anaesthesia and may include antibiotic therapy; treatment sessions typically last 1–2 hours and may be split over multiple visits. For advanced cases we coordinate care with a periodontist to ensure the best outcomes.

Benefits of Deep Cleaning

Deep cleaning can relieve sore, bleeding gums and is a first‑line treatment for mild to moderate gum disease. It eliminates bad breath, improves your oral and overall health, prevents tooth loss and gum recession, and reduces the risk of advanced periodontal disease. Many patients notice healthier gums and improved comfort after completing therapy.

Risks and Aftercare

Scaling and root planing are safe and effective, but minor side effects are possible. Some patients experience temporary bleeding, gum recession, infection, looseness of teeth, sensitivity or tenderness. Good oral hygiene and regular maintenance visits help minimise these risks and support healing. We’ll provide detailed aftercare instructions, including gentle brushing, flossing and the use of antimicrobial mouthwash. Regular checkups allow us to monitor your progress and recommend further treatment if needed.

Deep Cleaning Dentist in Dallas

Deep cleaning—also known as scaling and root planing—is an intensive procedure designed to remove stubborn plaque and tartar beneath the gumline. As a deep cleaning dentist in Dallas, we use specialized instruments to carefully clean and smooth the tooth roots, allowing gums to reattach and heal. This service is essential for patients with moderate to advanced gum disease and is complemented by local antibiotic therapy and regular maintenance visits.

Maintaining Healthy Gums

After treatment, daily brushing, flossing and regular dental visits are essential to keep gum disease from returning. We’ll provide personalized guidance on home care and recommend products to promote healing and health.
