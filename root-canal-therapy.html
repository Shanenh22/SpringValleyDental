What is Root Canal Therapy?

Root canal therapy is a procedure that removes infected or inflamed pulp tissue from inside your tooth. According to the Cleveland Clinic, root canals are performed when bacteria from deep cavities, cracks or injuries reach the pulp and cause pain and inflammation. By cleaning and sealing the canals, we stop the pain and prevent further infection while preserving your natural tooth structure. With modern techniques and local anaesthesia, root canals are comfortable and can often be completed in a single visit.

Why You Might Need a Root Canal

Signs that a root canal may be necessary include severe, lingering toothache pain; increased sensitivity to hot or cold; swelling or tenderness in the gums; darkening or discoloration of the tooth; and the presence of a pimple‑like bump (fistula) on the gums. These symptoms indicate that the pulp inside the tooth is infected or dying and needs to be removed to save the tooth.

Signs You Might Need a Root Canal

  • Severe, lingering toothache pain
  • Increased sensitivity to hot or cold temperatures
  • Swelling or tenderness in the gums
  • Darkening or discoloration of the tooth
  • A small bump or pimple on the gums near the tooth

Our Gentle Root Canal Process

During your appointment, we’ll numb the area completely and gently remove the damaged pulp. The Cleveland Clinic describes a typical root canal in several steps: after numbing, a small hole is drilled to access the pulp chamber; infected pulp and nerve tissue are removed; the canals are cleaned and shaped, then filled with a biocompatible material such as gutta‑percha and sealed. In most cases we then place a custom crown over the tooth to restore strength and appearance. We offer sedation options for anxious patients so you can relax throughout the procedure.

Benefits of Root Canal Therapy

Root canals remove infection and save your natural tooth, preventing the need for extraction. By preserving your tooth, we maintain proper chewing function and prevent neighbouring teeth from shifting. Root canal therapy also stops infection from spreading and alleviates pain.

Aftercare & Recovery

It’s normal to experience mild soreness for a few days after a root canal, but over‑the‑counter pain relievers and a soft diet usually help. Most people recover within a week. Avoid smoking and stick to a soft diet until discomfort subsides, and maintain excellent oral hygiene to support healing. We’ll provide detailed instructions on caring for your tooth, and most patients return to normal activities quickly. Maintaining good oral hygiene and regular checkups will help your treated tooth last for many years.
